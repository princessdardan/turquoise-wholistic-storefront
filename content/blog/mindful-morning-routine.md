How you begin your morning sets the trajectory for your entire day. A mindful morning routine does not have to be elaborate or time-consuming — even 20 minutes of intentional practice can transform your energy, focus, and emotional resilience.

## The Science of Morning Routines

Our cortisol levels naturally peak within 30–60 minutes of waking (the cortisol awakening response). By aligning our morning activities with this biological rhythm, we can harness this natural energy surge rather than fighting against it.

## Building Your Morning Practice

### 1. Hydrate First

Before coffee, before anything else — drink a glass of water. After 7–8 hours without fluids, your body is mildly dehydrated. Adding a squeeze of lemon provides vitamin C and supports digestion.

### 2. Breathwork (5 minutes)

Simple breathing exercises activate the parasympathetic nervous system and centre your mind:

- **Box breathing:** Inhale for 4 counts, hold for 4, exhale for 4, hold for 4. Repeat 4–6 cycles.
- **4-7-8 breathing:** Inhale for 4 counts, hold for 7, exhale slowly for 8. Repeat 3 times.

### 3. Gentle Movement (10 minutes)

You do not need a full workout. Gentle stretching, yoga, or a short walk outdoors accomplishes the goal of waking up your body:

- Sun salutations (yoga)
- A walk around the block in natural light
- Simple stretching focusing on areas that feel tight

### 4. Nourishing Breakfast

Fuel your body with a balanced meal that includes:

- **Protein** — eggs, Greek yogourt, or a plant-based protein smoothie
- **Healthy fats** — avocado, nuts, or chia seeds
- **Complex carbohydrates** — oats, whole grain toast, or sweet potato

### 5. Set an Intention

Before diving into tasks, take one minute to set a simple intention for the day. This could be a word (patience, focus, joy), a goal, or an affirmation. Writing it down reinforces the commitment.

## What to Avoid

- **Checking your phone immediately** — this floods your brain with external stimuli before you have centred yourself
- **Caffeine on an empty stomach** — coffee stimulates acid production and can spike cortisol; eat something first
- **Rushing** — if your morning feels frantic, wake up 15 minutes earlier; the calm is worth it

## Making It Stick

Start small. Choose just one or two practices and commit to them for 21 days. Once they feel natural, add another. The goal is not perfection — it is consistency. Even on busy mornings, a single minute of intentional breathing is better than skipping your routine entirely.

Your morning is the one part of your day that belongs entirely to you. Treat it as a gift.
