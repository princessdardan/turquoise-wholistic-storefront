Essential oils have been used for thousands of years across cultures for their therapeutic properties. Whether you are looking to improve sleep, ease tension, or simply create a calming atmosphere at home, aromatherapy can be a wonderful starting point.

## What Are Essential Oils?

Essential oils are concentrated plant extracts obtained through steam distillation or cold pressing. Each oil contains the "essence" of its source plant — the unique combination of aromatic compounds that gives the plant its characteristic scent and properties.

## Five Must-Have Essential Oils

### 1. Lavender

The most versatile essential oil. Known for its calming properties, lavender can help with sleep, stress relief, and minor skin irritations. It is gentle enough for most people and blends well with almost any other oil.

### 2. Peppermint

Refreshing and invigorating, peppermint is excellent for mental alertness, headache relief, and digestive comfort. A few drops in a diffuser can transform your workspace.

### 3. Tea Tree

A powerhouse for natural cleaning and skin care. Tea tree oil has well-documented antimicrobial properties, making it a staple for DIY cleaning sprays and blemish treatments.

### 4. Eucalyptus

Perfect for respiratory support, especially during cold and flu season. Add a few drops to a bowl of steaming water for a soothing inhalation experience.

### 5. Lemon

Bright and uplifting, lemon oil is wonderful for mood enhancement and natural cleaning. Its fresh scent pairs beautifully with other oils for custom blends.

## Safety First

Essential oils are potent and should be used with care:

- **Always dilute** before applying to skin (use a carrier oil like jojoba or coconut)
- **Never ingest** essential oils unless directed by a qualified practitioner
- **Patch test** new oils on a small area of skin before wider use
- **Keep away** from eyes, inner ears, and sensitive areas
- **Store properly** in dark glass bottles away from heat and sunlight

## Getting Started

Invest in a quality ultrasonic diffuser and start with just two or three oils. Experiment with single oils before trying blends, and keep a journal of what works best for you. Remember, less is more when it comes to essential oils — a little goes a long way.
