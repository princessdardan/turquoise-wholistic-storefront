Quality sleep is the foundation of good health, yet millions of Canadians struggle to get the rest they need. Before reaching for pharmaceutical sleep aids, consider these natural approaches that address the root causes of sleeplessness.

## 1. Magnesium

Often called "nature's relaxation mineral," magnesium plays a role in over 300 enzymatic reactions in the body, including those that regulate the nervous system and promote relaxation.

**How it helps:** Magnesium activates the parasympathetic nervous system, helps regulate melatonin production, and binds to GABA receptors — the same neurotransmitter targeted by many sleep medications.

**Best forms for sleep:** Magnesium glycinate (gentle on the stomach and well-absorbed) or magnesium threonate (crosses the blood-brain barrier).

**Suggested use:** 200–400 mg taken 30–60 minutes before bed.

## 2. Valerian Root

This flowering plant has been used as a sleep aid since ancient Greece and Rome. Modern research supports its traditional use, showing improvements in sleep quality and time to fall asleep.

**How it helps:** Valerian appears to increase GABA levels in the brain, promoting calmness and reducing the time it takes to fall asleep.

**Suggested use:** 300–600 mg of valerian root extract, taken 30 minutes to two hours before bed. Effects may take 2–4 weeks of consistent use to fully manifest.

## 3. Chamomile Tea

A cup of chamomile tea before bed is more than just a comforting ritual. The flavonoid apigenin in chamomile binds to benzodiazepine receptors in the brain, producing a mild sedative effect.

**How it helps:** Beyond its direct calming effects, the ritual of preparing and sipping warm tea signals to your body that it is time to wind down.

**Suggested use:** Steep 2–3 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers (or one tea bag) in hot water for 5–10 minutes. Drink 30–45 minutes before bed.

## 4. Lavender Aromatherapy

The scent of lavender has been shown in multiple studies to improve sleep quality, increase deep sleep time, and reduce nighttime wakefulness.

**How it helps:** Inhaled lavender compounds interact with the limbic system, the brain's emotional centre, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety.

**Suggested use:** Add 3–5 drops of pure lavender essential oil to a bedside diffuser, or place a sachet of dried lavender under your pillow.

## 5. Sleep Hygiene Practices

No supplement can overcome poor sleep habits. These evidence-based practices create the conditions for restorative sleep:

- **Consistent schedule** — go to bed and wake up at the same time daily, even on weekends
- **Cool, dark room** — aim for 16–19°C (60–67°F) and use blackout curtains
- **Screen curfew** — stop using phones, tablets, and computers at least one hour before bed
- **Limit caffeine** — avoid caffeine after 2 PM (it has a half-life of 5–6 hours)
- **Wind-down routine** — establish a relaxing 30-minute pre-bed ritual

## Combining Approaches

Natural sleep remedies often work best in combination. Try pairing magnesium with chamomile tea, or valerian root with lavender aromatherapy. Keep a sleep diary to track what works best for you, and remember that consistency is key — give each approach at least two weeks before evaluating its effectiveness.

*Always consult a healthcare practitioner if you experience chronic insomnia, as it may indicate an underlying health condition that requires professional attention.*
